\section{Introduction}

Sometimes, only a subset of axioms of a theory are required to prove
of a theorem.  When that is the case, selecting beforehand such subset
can provide a tremendous boost during proof search.  The question then
becomes how to find such subset.  In this document we show how
Probabilistic Logic Networks (PLN for short) can be used to formulate
the problem of attention allocation and how to search such subsets via
PLN reasoning.
